## üèõÔ∏è Deputy Chairman of Rajya Sabha (Article 90-92)

<details>
<summary><strong>1. Who elects the Deputy Chairman of Rajya Sabha?</strong></summary>

- He/She is elected from amongst the members of the **Rajya Sabha** only.
- Must be a member of Rajya Sabha.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>2. What is the process for the removal of the Deputy Chairman?</strong></summary>

- **Majority:** Effective Majority (Majority of total seats filled).
- **Notice:** Resolution requires **14 days** prior notice.
- **Resignation:** Submits resignation to the **Chairman** of Rajya Sabha.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>3. Can the Deputy Chairman preside over the house while their removal is under consideration? (Article 92)</strong></summary>

- **No**, they cannot preside.
- They sit as a normal member.
- Note: The Chairman has the right to speak during their own removal proceedings.
</details>

## üó£Ô∏è Speaker & Deputy Speaker (Article 93)

<details>
<summary><strong>1. To whom do the Speaker and Deputy Speaker submit their resignations?</strong></summary>

- **Speaker** resigns to ‚Üí **Deputy Speaker**.
- **Deputy Speaker** resigns to ‚Üí **Speaker**.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>2. What is the specific majority required to remove the Speaker?</strong></summary>

- **Effective Majority** (Majority of the "then" members of Lok Sabha).
- Requires **14 days** prior notice.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>3. What is the status of the Deputy Speaker post since 2019?</strong></summary>

- It has been **vacant**.
- The Delhi High Court has asked Parliament to elect one.
</details>

## ‚è±Ô∏è Pro-tem Speaker

<details>
<summary><strong>1. Who appoints the Pro-tem Speaker and who usually gets the post?</strong></summary>

- Appointed by: **The President**.
- Convention: Usually the **seniormost member** of the Lok Sabha.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>2. What are the three main duties of the Pro-tem Speaker?</strong></summary>

1.  Administer **oaths** to new MPs.
2.  Conduct elections for the **new Speaker**.
3.  Conduct the **floor test** (majority test).
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>3. When does the office of Pro-tem Speaker cease to exist?</strong></summary>

- It ceases to exist automatically when the **new Speaker is elected** by the House.
</details>

## ‚öîÔ∏è Powers, Rules & Suspension

<details>
<summary><strong>1. Role of Speaker regarding Money Bills and Defection?</strong></summary>

- **Money Bill:** Speaker has the **final authority** to certify if a bill is a Money Bill.
- **Defection:** Can disqualify members under **Schedule X** (Anti-defection Law).
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>2. What is Rule 374A of Lok Sabha?</strong></summary>

- Added in 2001.
- Empowers the Speaker to **automatically suspend** an MP for a maximum of **5 days** for disrupting business.
- Used by Sumitra Mahajan in 2015 to suspend 25 Congress MPs.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>3. What is the "Panel of Presiding Officers"?</strong></summary>

- Nominated by the Speaker (LS) from members (max 10).
- Any of them can preside in the **absence** of Speaker/Deputy Speaker.
- **Crucial Note:** They **CANNOT** preside if the office of Speaker/Deputy Speaker is **vacant** (in that case, President appoints someone).
</details>

---

## üó≥Ô∏è Voting & Quorum (Article 100)

<details>
<summary><strong>1. What is the Quorum required to run the house?</strong></summary>

- **10%** of the total members.
- **Lok Sabha:** ~54 members.
- **Rajya Sabha:** ~25 members.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>2. When do the Speaker/Chairman vote in the house?</strong></summary>

- They exercise a **Casting Vote** only in the case of a **tie**.
</details>

---

## üö´ Disqualification & Vacation (Article 101-103)

<details>
<summary><strong>1. What happens if an MP is absent without permission? (Article 101)</strong></summary>

- If absent for more than **60 days**, they will be removed from their seat.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>2. What are the grounds for disqualification under Article 102?</strong></summary>

1.  Holding an **Office of Profit** (unless exempted).
2.  Being of **Unsound Mind** (declared by court).
3.  Being an **Undischarged Insolvent**.
4.  Voluntarily acquiring **foreign citizenship**.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>3. Who decides on disqualification under Article 102 vs Schedule X?</strong></summary>

- **Article 102 (Office of Profit, etc.):** Decided by **President** (on opinion of Election Commission).
- **Schedule X (Defection):** Decided by the **Speaker/Chairman**.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>4. What is the "Jaya Bachchan Case 2006" related to?</strong></summary>

- It laid down tests for **Office of Profit**:
    1. Pecuniary gains (receiving income).
    2. Executive nature of office.
    3. Exercise of executive powers.
</details>

Deputy Chairman of the Rajya Sabha:
‚ùñ He acts as the chairman in the absence of the Chairman.
‚ùñ The Office of Deputy Chairman is to ensure the continuity of the Rajya Sabha.
‚ùñ While acting as the chairman, he enjoys all the benefits of the chairman.
‚ùñ Deputy chairman is elected from the Rajya Sabha only.
‚ùñ Deputy Chairman is a member of the Rajya Sabha.
## Article 90:
‚ùñ A Deputy chairman cannot continue in his post if he/she is no longer a member of the parliament.
‚ùñ The  Deputy  Chairman  submits  his resignation  to  the  Chairman  of  the  Rajya  Sabha.  (The  Chairman
resigns to the President).
‚ùñ The Deputy Chairman can be removed from his post by the majority of the members of the house (majority
of total seats filled). It is also called the effective majority.
‚ùñ For his removal, a resolution has to be moved in the house with at least 14 days of prior notice.
## Article 92:
‚ùñ Whenever the removal of the Vice President is under consideration, he cannot preside over the proceedings
of the house.
‚ùñ Whenever the removal of the Deputy Chairman is in consideration, he will sit as a member only.
‚ùñ The Chairman, during the consideration for removal will have the right to speak in the house.
## Article 93 - Speaker/ Deputy Speaker:
‚ùñ Both speaker and deputy speaker are constitutional posts. Thay are to be elected by the house.
‚ùñ Election  of  Speaker: The  speaker  is  to  be  elected  from  amongst  the  members  of  the  Lok  Sabha  by  a
majority vote.
‚ùñ Removal: S/he  can  be  removed  by  the  majority  of  the  then  members  of  Lok  Sabha  (effective  majority).
However, 14 days prior notice is required in this case.
‚ùñ Resignation: The speaker submits his resignation to the Deputy Speaker and the Deputy Speaker submits his
resignation to the Speaker.
‚ùñ Cease: They will cease to hold their post if they are not members of Lok Sabha.
## Note:
‚ùñ The  post  of deputy  speaker  has  been  vacant  since  2019.  The  Delhi  High  Court has  asked  the  Parliament  to
elect the deputy speaker.

## Pro-tem Speaker:
‚ùñ Pro-tem  is  a  Latin  phrase  which  means ‚Äúfor the time being‚Äù. The Protem Speaker  is  a  temporary  speaker
appointed for a limited period of time.
‚ùñ As provided by the Constitution, the Speaker of the last Lok Sabha vacates his office immediately before the
first  meeting  of  the  newly-elected  Lok  Sabha.  Therefore, the  President  appoints  a  member  of  the  Lok
Sabha as the Speaker Pro tem.
‚ùñ Usually, the seniormost member is selected for the post.
‚ùñ The President himself administers the oath to the Speaker Pro Tem.
‚ùñ The  Speaker  Pro  Tem  has  all  the  powers  of  the  Speaker.  He presides  over  the  first  sitting  of  the  newly-
elected Lok Sabha.
‚ùñ His main duty is
‚û¢ To administer oaths to the new members (MPs).
‚û¢ To conduct elections of the new Speaker.
‚û¢ To conduct the floor test (majority test).
‚ùñ When the new Speaker is elected by the House, the office of the Speaker Pro Tem ceases to exist. Hence, this
office is a temporary office, existing for a few days.
Role of Speaker:
‚ùñ He/she is the presiding officer of the house.
‚ùñ He/she has the final authority to decide if the bill is a money bill or not.
‚ùñ He runs the house according to the rules of Lok Sabha.
‚ùñ He /she can suspend any member of the Lok Sabha for misconduct. (same power to the chairman of the
## Rajya Sabha)
‚ùñ He/she  can  also disqualify  any  member  under  Anti-defection  Law (Schedule  X).  (same  power  to the
chairman of the rajya sabha).
‚ùñ He is the guardian of powers and privileges of the members, the House as a whole and its committees.
‚ùñ He is the principal spokesman of the House, and his decision in all Parliamentary matters is final. He is
thus much more than merely the presiding officer of the Lok Sabha. He is the supreme authority within the
## House.
‚ùñ The Speaker of the Lok Sabha derives his powers and duties from three sources, that is, the Constitution of
India,  the  Rules  of  Procedure  and  Conduct  of  Business  of  Lok  Sabha,  and  Parliamentary  Conventions
(residuary powers that are unwritten or unspecified in the Rules)



## 4
Rules for the Lok Sabha:
‚ùñ MPs are required to adhere to certain rules of parliamentary etiquette.
‚û¢ For  example,  the  Lok  Sabha rulebook  specifies  that  MPs  are  not  to  interrupt  the  speech  of  others,
maintain silence and not obstruct proceedings by hissing or making running commentaries during
debates.
‚ùñ Newer forms of protest led to these rules being updated in 1989.
‚ùñ Now, members cannot shout slogans, display placards, tear up documents in protest, and play a cassette or a
tape recorder in the House.
‚ùñ Rajya Sabha has similar rules.
‚ùñ To  conduct  the  proceedings  smoothly,  the  rulebook  also  gives  certain  similar  powers  to  the  presiding
officers of both Houses.
‚ùñ The  presiding  officer  of  each  House  can direct  an  MP  to  withdraw  from  the  legislative  chamber for
grossly disorderly conduct.
‚ùñ The MP then has to remain absent from the proceedings of the House for the remainder of the day.
‚ùñ The presiding officers can also ‚Äúname‚Äù an MP for ‚Äúpersistently and wilfully obstructing the business‚Äù
of the House.
‚ùñ In such a case, usually, the Parliamentary Affairs Minister moves a motion for suspending the offending
MP from the service of the House. The suspension can last until the end of the session.
Rules Regarding Suspension of the Members:
‚ùñ The chairman may, if he deems it necessary, name a member who disregards the authority of the Chair
or abuses the rules of the council by persistently and willfully obstructing the business thereof.
‚ùñ If  a  member  is  so  named  by  the  Chairman,  he  shall  forthwith  put  question  on  a  motion  being  made,  no
amendment,  adjournment  or  debate  being  allowed,  that  the  member  (naming  him)  be  suspended  from  the
service of the Council for a period not exceeding the remainder of the Session:
‚û¢ Provided  that  the  council  may,  at  any  time,  on  a  motion  being  made  resolve  that  such  suspension  be
terminated.
‚û¢ A member suspended under this rule shall forthwith quit the precincts of the council.
Lok Sabha Rule for Suspension:
‚ùñ In 2001, the Lok Sabha rule was amended to give the Speaker one additional power.
‚ùñ A new rule, 374A, empowers the Speaker to automatically suspend an MP for a maximum of five days
for disrupting the business of the House.
‚û¢ ‚û¢ In 2015, Speaker Sumitra Mahajan used this rule to suspend 25 Congress MPs.

Disruption and Suspension:
‚ùñ The   first   instance   occurred   in 1963. A   few   Lok   Sabha   MPs   first   interrupted   President   Sarvapalli
Radhakrishnan and then walked out while he was delivering the joint address to both Houses.
‚ùñ Lok  Sabha  ended  up  reprimanding  these  MPs.  In 1989,  63  MPs  were  suspended  from  Lok  Sabha  on  the
discussion of the Thakar Commission report.
‚ùñ More recently in 2010, 7  MPs  were  suspended  from  Rajya  Sabha for snatching the women‚Äôs reservation
bill from the minister.
‚ùñ Since then, MPs have raised slogans, used pepper spray in the House and displayed placards.
‚ùñ Former  President  K  R  Narayanan, who also chaired Rajya Sabha from 1992-97, explained the difficulty
involved:
‚ùñ ‚ÄúIn most cases, disorders in the House arise out of a sense of frustration felt by members due to lack of
opportunities  to  make  his  point,  or  clear  his  chest  of  grievances  of  the  people  that  move  him  or  out  of  the
heat  of  the  moment.  They  are  perhaps  easier  to  deal  with.  What  is  more  difficult  to  tackle  is  planned
parliamentary offenses and deliberate disturbances for publicity or for political motives‚Äù
‚ùñ In  2001,  during  a  national  conference  in  the Central Hall  of  Parliament organised  on  the  issue  of
disruptions, different political leaders summed up their positions.
‚ùñ Prime  Minister Atal  Bihari  Vajpayee said  the  majority  party  is  responsible  for  governing  and  should  take
other parties into confidence.
‚ùñ He  said  the  Opposition should  play  a  constructive  role  in  Parliament and  be  allowed  to  put  forward  its
views and express itself in a dignified manner.
Panel of Presiding Officers (Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha):
‚ùñ This idea of a panel of presiding officers is not mentioned in the constitution.
‚ùñ Under  the Rules  of  Lok  Sabha, the  Speaker  nominates  from  amongst  the  members a  panel  of  not  more

## Deputy Speaker.

‚ùñ He holds office until a new panel of chairpersons is nominated.
‚ùñ When  a  member  of  the  panel  of  chairpersons  is  also  not present, any  other  person  as  determined  by  the

‚ùñ Note: It  must  be emphasized  here  that  a  member  of  the  panel  of  chairpersons cannot  preside  over  the

‚û¢ During such time, the Speaker‚Äôs duties are to be performed by such members of the House as the

Article 100 - Voting in the House:
‚ùñ All  matters  in  the  House  are  decided  by  the majority  of  present  and  voting (simple  majority). Example:
The passing of bills, except constitutional amendment bill
‚ùñ Speaker and Chairman: They will always have casting vote (will vote only in case of a tie).
‚ùñ Quorum (minimum strength required to start a house): Ideally 10% of the members are required to start

‚ùñ If the Quorum is not fulfilled, then the sitting of the house will be suspended by the Presiding officer.
## Article 101:
‚ùñ No person can be a member of both houses at the same time.
‚ùñ If an MLA is elected as an MP, then he has to resign from his MLA post, otherwise, his MP seat will become
vacant.
‚ùñ An MP can resign to a chairman or the speaker of the respective house.
‚ùñ The 33rd  Amendment added  a  point  in  Article  101  and gave  ultimate  power  to  the  chairman or  the
speaker with regard to the resignation of the MPs. Indira Gandhi added this inorder to save her government

‚ùñ Here,  the chairman/speaker  can  deny  such  resignation if  he  is  satisfied  that  the  resignation  is  not
voluntary or genuine.
‚ùñ The Supreme Court in various cases has held that this power of the speaker/chairman should not be used
against the will of the MPs.
‚ùñ If any MP is absent from the house for more than 60 days without permission, he will be removed from
his seat.

‚û¢ Undischarged insolvent: if a person is of undischarged insolvent,  eg Vijay Malaya.
‚û¢ Unsoundness of Mind: if he is of unsound mind and stands so declared by a court.
